Why make a blog?

Honestly, I just thought it'd be a neat thing to have. I saw a bunch of other personal websites that had them, and I was like - "why not? I can do that!"

Turns out, writing a blog is hard. So I guess a better way to describe this is a collection of random thoughts and ideas that I felt like putting down somewhere, for fear of losing them. In any case it's probably a half-decent way to keep my writing skills sharp (If I have any left at all after not writing anything past high school).

How will this blog evolve?

No clue! Again, this is most likely just going to be a repository for my thoughts, and I don't (currently) have any long term plans for it. I could write about anything from my hobbies to some of the projects I'm working on, or maybe some new technologies that I found interesting. It's really up in the air, but I think that's part of the fun! You don't really know what's coming next unless you check back in (hint hint).

Why should you bother reading?

Tell you what, I can't really think of a good reason. Maybe to get a better sense of who I am? Maybe if you want to know how I think? Or, if you're one of my friends who somehow managed to find this and want to dig around, feel free.

But, I will say this: Why do you need a reason to read this? Hell, why do you need a reason to read/do anything? Not everything really needs to have a purpose, or a set goal - I think that's perfectly fine. Sometimes you just want to do something for the sake of doing it, and I think that's a perfectly valid reason. So, if you want to read this, go ahead! If not, that's cool too. I'll keep writing it either way.

After doing some reflection, though, I can think of a few reasons why I personally find this blog useful:

  1. Writing things down is immensively helpful, for me and (probably) you. I don't think it's the act of writing, per se, but actually just sitting down for a bit, no distractions, with nothing but your thoughts? We don't really get an opportunity to do that in our day-to-day lives, and I think it's a shame. It's a great way to deal with stress, anxiety, and basically whatever else comes up. Hell, one time I sat for 10 minutes reflecting on what type of food I wanted to make for dinner. It ended up tasting pretty good, so I guess it worked?

  2. Writing is a muscle, and like any muscle, it needs to be exercised. Cliche much? But seriously, I wouldn't call myself the best writer. I think I do alright when talking in person, but writing? That's a whole different ballgame. You need to somehow convey your thoughts clearly, but also keep the reader engaged, keep it short (Which I'm already failing at), and make sure you don't sound like a complete idiot. And then there's the whole thing about crafting a unique style, which I think is the hardest part. But it's not like anybody ever got better at writing by not writing, right? So, might as well start.

  3. My memory sucks. I'm working on it, but I think a large part of it is just writing stuff down. For me, and honestly, for a lot of people, our lives are just getting wayy too complicated and busy. Hell, I'm using Google Calendar to schedule hangouts with my friends, which 2 years ago I would have thought was ridiculous. But, here we are. Writing stuff down, whether it be an event, a meeting, or even just random thoughts, helps me with that. And it might just help you too.

What's with the banner?

Good question! I say this like you asked me and not like I wrote this because I wanted to. Anyways! I was wondering what to put as the banner for this post when I realized: it's my blog, I can do whatever I want! So, I chose an image of one of my favorite animated movies of all time, How to Train Your Dragon. If you haven't watched it, get on it. The second one is probably my favorite. Maybe I'll keep this theme going for a bit, I like it.

Conclusion

I thought this would be a good section to have. Since these writing sessions aren't really planned, I think it'd be good for me to reread what I wrote and just summarize it. If not for you, then at least for future me when I look back at this and wonder what was going through my head. Anyways, here's the key points:

  1. I now have a blog, yay! I can stop saying "I should make a blog".
  2. I don't know what this blog is going to be about, but I think that's part of the fun.
  3. I think writing is a great way to deal with stress & anxiety, while simultaneously improving your writing skills and helping you remember things.
  4. I like How to Train Your Dragon, and I think you should watch it if you haven't already.

See you next time!
